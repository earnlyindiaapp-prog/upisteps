65. Credit Score: How to Maintain 750+ and Get Cheaper Loans

A 750+ Credit Score = Lower Interest + Faster Loan Approval

Banks check your CIBIL score before giving any loan. Range is 300 to 900. 750+ score can save you 2% interest. On a 10 Lakh loan, that's 2 Lakh saved. Here's how to build and maintain it in 2026.

What is a Credit Score?

It's your "financial report card". Banks check: Do you pay EMIs on time? How much debt do you have? How many credit cards?

750 - 900 Excellent → Cheapest loan rates
650 - 749 Good → Loan approved, higher interest
300 - 649 Poor → Loan difficult or very expensive

5 Factors That Affect Your Score

  • 35% - Payment History: Did you pay EMIs and bills on time
  • 30% - Credit Utilization: How much of your limit you use
  • 15% - Length of Credit History: How long you've had credit
  • 10% - Credit Mix: Cards + Home Loan + Car Loan
  • 10% - New Credit: How many times you applied for loans

7 Golden Rules for 750+ Score

Rule 1: Pay Bills On Time

Even 1 day late hurts your score. Set up auto-debit for credit cards and EMIs

Rule 2: Keep Utilization Below 30%

If limit is 1 Lakh, don't spend more than 30,000
If spending is high, pay bill twice a month

Rule 3: Don't Close Old Cards

Old card = Long history = Better score. Use it once a year to keep it active

Rule 4: Limit Loan Applications

Every "hard inquiry" drops score by 5 points. Apply only when needed

Benefits of a Good Score

1. Lower Interest: 8.5% instead of 10.5% on home loan
2. Faster Approval: Loan in 24 hours
3. Higher Limit: 2x Credit Card limit
4. Waived Fees: Banks will offer fee waivers

How to Check Score for Free

Apps: CIBIL, Experian, Paytm, PhonePe, Bank apps. You get 1 free report every year.

Final Thought: A credit score isn't built in a day. Small habits for 12 months = 750+. Check yours today.

The 3 Main Types of Insurance

72. How to Read a Company Balance Sheet in 5 Minutes

A balance sheet tells you if a company is healthy or about to die.

You don't need to be a CA to read it. If you're buying stocks, you must check 3 things in 5 minutes: Assets, Liabilities, and Debt. Here's the beginner's cheat sheet.

What is a Balance Sheet?

It's a photo of the company's finances on one day.
Formula: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
Think of it like: What you OWN = What you OWE + What's actually yours

The 3 Parts You Must Check

1. Assets - "What the Company Owns"

  • Current Assets: Cash, Inventory, Money customers owe. Should be high
  • Fixed Assets: Factories, Buildings, Machines. Long term

Good Sign: Cash is increasing every year

2. Liabilities - "What the Company Owes"

  • Current Liabilities: Bills to pay in 1 year. Salary, vendors
  • Long Term Debt: Bank loans, bonds. This is the dangerous one

Bad Sign: Debt is more than 50% of total assets

3. Shareholder's Equity - "What's Actually Yours"

  • Money left for shareholders after paying all debt
  • If this is growing, company is creating value

Good Sign: Equity growing 15%+ every year

5-Minute Health Check for Any Company

Check Good Bad
Debt to Equity Less than 1 More than 2
Current Ratio More than 1.5 Less than 1
Cash Increasing yearly Always borrowing

Red Flags - Run Away

  • Too much debt: Company pays interest instead of growing
  • Negative Equity: Liabilities > Assets. Company is technically bankrupt
  • Inventory piling up: Can't sell products
  • Auditor changed suddenly: Something is wrong

Where to Find Balance Sheet for Free

Websites: Screener.in, Moneycontrol, Company website → Investor Relations → Annual Report

Example: Good vs Bad Company

Good Company: Assets ₹100 Cr, Debt ₹20 Cr, Cash ₹15 Cr, Equity growing

Bad Company: Assets ₹100 Cr, Debt ₹90 Cr, Cash ₹1 Cr, Equity falling

Final Thought: Don't buy a company with weak balance sheet, even if stock is cheap. Debt kills companies in recession. Always check: Is the company rich or just pretending to be rich?

74. How to File ITR Yourself in 2026: Step by Step Guide

CA charges ₹2000. You can do it yourself in 20 minutes for FREE.

Filing ITR is now super easy. Govt portal + AI tools do 90% of the work. If you have salary income, this guide will help you file ITR-1 in 7 steps without any CA.

Who Should File ITR-1 in 2026?

  • Income: Up to ₹50 Lakhs
  • Income Sources: Salary, 1 House Property, Other sources like FD interest
  • Not Applicable: Business income, Capital gains, More than 1 house

Deadline: 31st July 2026 for AY 2026-27

Documents Needed - Keep Ready

  1. Form 16: From your company
  2. AIS + 26AS: Download from Income Tax portal. Shows all TDS
  3. Bank Statements: For interest income
  4. Investment Proofs: 80C, 80D receipts
  5. PAN + Aadhaar: Must be linked

7 Steps to File ITR Yourself

Step 1: Login to Income Tax Portal

Go to incometax.gov.in → Login with PAN → Password → OTP

Step 2: Go to "File ITR"

Dashboard → e-File → Income Tax Return → File New ITR → Assessment Year 2026-27

Step 3: Select ITR Form

Choose ITR-1 for Salary. Select Old or New Regime. Portal will show which is better.

Step 4: Fill Income Details

Most data is pre-filled from Form 16 and AIS.
Just verify: Salary, Other Income, Deductions. Edit if needed.

Step 5: Claim Deductions

80C: PPF, ELSS, LIC
80D: Health Insurance
HRA: Rent receipts
Home Loan: Interest certificate

Step 6: Pay Tax if Any

If tax due > ₹10,000, pay via "e-Pay Tax". Use netbanking/UPI. Save challan number.

Step 7: Preview + Submit + e-Verify

Preview ITR → Submit → e-Verify within 30 days
Best way: Aadhaar OTP. Takes 10 seconds. Done!

Old vs New Regime - Portal Tells You

In Step 3, the portal automatically calculates tax for BOTH regimes. Pick the one with lower tax. You can change every year if salaried.

3 Common Mistakes

  • Not e-Verifying: ITR is invalid without e-Verification
  • Wrong bank account: Refund will fail. Verify account in portal first
  • Ignoring AIS: If Form 16 and AIS don't match, use AIS data

What After Filing?

  1. Download ITR-V and Acknowledgement
  2. CPC will process in 30-45 days
  3. Refund comes directly to bank in 4-6 weeks

Final Thought: Don't wait till 30th July. File in June. Portal is less crowded and you get refund faster. 20 minutes now can save you ₹2000 CA fees + penalty later.

75. Credit Card Mistakes: How Banks Make ₹1 Lakh From You

Credit card is a tool. Use it right = Free money. Use it wrong = Debt trap.

Banks make money when you make mistakes. Late fees, interest, annual charges. An average person loses ₹50,000 to ₹1 Lakh in 5 years. Here are 7 mistakes to avoid in 2026 and how to use cards like a pro.

The 7 Deadly Credit Card Mistakes

Mistake 1: Paying Only Minimum Due

Bill: ₹50,000 Min Due: ₹5,000
Interest: 3.5% per month = 42% per year
Result: That ₹50,000 becomes ₹1.2 Lakhs in 2 years

Mistake 2: Late Payment

Fee: ₹500 to ₹1300 + Interest + Credit Score damage
Fix: Set auto-pay for "Total Amount Due"

Mistake 3: Cash Withdrawal

Fee: 2.5% + ₹10 Interest: 3.5%/month from Day 1. No grace period
₹10,000 cash = ₹14,200 in 12 months

Mistake 4: EMI on Everything

12% to 15% interest on EMI. A ₹40,000 phone on EMI = ₹47,000
Fix: Save and buy. Or use 0% EMI only on big purchases

Mistake 5: Ignoring Annual Fees

Paying ₹999/year for a card you don't use.
Fix: Call and ask for "fee waiver" or downgrade to free card

Mistake 6: Maxing Out Limit

Using >30% of limit hurts credit score. Using 90% = Loan rejection
Fix: Keep usage below 30%

78. SIP vs Lumpsum: Which Makes More Money in 2026?

Got ₹5 Lakhs? Invest all at once or ₹10K every month? The answer will surprise you.

This is the #1 question in mutual funds. SIP is for salaried people. Lumpsum is for bonus/inheritance. Let's see which one actually makes more money with real data.

What is SIP and Lumpsum?

SIP - Systematic Investment Plan

Invest small amount every month. Example: ₹10,000 every 1st of month. Auto-debit.

Lumpsum

Invest big amount one time. Example: ₹5,00,000 all at once.

Head to Head: ₹5 Lakhs Over 10 Years @ 12%

Method Investment Maturity
Lumpsum ₹5 Lakhs ₹5,00,000 ₹15,52,000
SIP ₹4,167/month ₹5,00,000 ₹9,18,000

Winner: Lumpsum by ₹6.3 Lakhs - Because money gets more time to compound

But Wait... Market Falls?

Lumpsum wins in rising market. SIP wins in falling/volatile market.

SIP Advantage: Rupee Cost Averaging

  • When market falls, SIP buys more units
  • When market rises, SIP buys less units
  • Average cost comes down. Less risk

Lumpsum Risk

  • If you invest at market peak, you may wait 2-3 years to recover
  • Needs strong stomach to see portfolio in red

The Smart 2026 Strategy

  1. If you have Salary: Do SIP. It's disciplined and automatic
  2. If you got Bonus/Inheritance: Don't dump all at once. Use "STP"
  3. STP - Systematic Transfer Plan: Park lumpsum in Liquid Fund. Transfer ₹40K/month to Equity MF for 12 months
  4. If Market crashes 20%: Put extra lumpsum. Buy the dip

Real Data: 2008 to 2026

Who made more money?

SIP Investor: Started ₹10K SIP in Jan 2008. Faced 50% crash. Still made 14% CAGR.
Lumpsum Investor: Invested ₹10 Lakhs in Jan 2008. Took 5 years to break even.

Conclusion: SIP saved the investor in 2008

SIP vs Lumpsum - Quick Decision

Choose SIP If Choose Lumpsum/STP If
Salaried, Monthly income Got bonus, inheritance
Can't handle market volatility Market is 20% down
Long term goal 10+ years You are experienced investor

3 Mistakes to Avoid

  • Stopping SIP when market falls: That's when SIP buys cheapest
  • Waiting for "perfect time" for lumpsum: Time in market > Timing the market
  • Comparing 1 year returns: Judge only after 5+ years

Final Thought: Lumpsum makes more money mathematically. SIP makes more money emotionally. If you have ₹5 Lakhs today, do STP over 12 months. If you earn monthly, do SIP and forget it. Consistency beats timing.
